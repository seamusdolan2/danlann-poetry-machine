# D Á N L A N N

*The Poetry Machine*

---

pronounced *dawn-lahn*

</div>

<br/>

### THE NAME

*Dán* is the Irish word for both *poem* and *fate* — the language does not distinguish between the two. *-Lann* means *place of*.

Dánlann means "the place of poems" and "the place of fate" simultaneously.

It is built the way Irish has always built its institutions: *leabharlann* (library), *amharclann* (theater), *pictiúrlann* (cinema). Dánlann adds a new room to that tradition — a digital one.

### THE ECHO

The surname Dolan comes from *Ó Dubhláin*: *dúbh* (dark) + *lán* (full). Say them aloud together — *Dánlann, Dolan* — and the echo is there without being forced. Same rhythm, same vowel weight. The name carries its maker inside it.

### WHAT IT IS

Dánlann is a single dark page. One quote appears at a time. Scroll, and it dissolves — replaced by another, chosen at random. The quotes span thousands of years: ancient Greek poetry, Zen koans, Stoic philosophy, hip-hop verses, passages from novels, and original writing. Each quote arrives through a different animation — smoke dissolving, ink bleeding, light burning through paper, a stone skipping across water. Nothing repeats the same way twice.

There is no navigation, no menu, no header. Just words appearing and disappearing in a dark room. A museum with one wall.

### THE ENGLISH NAME

*The Poetry Machine* is the plain-language handle. It has a blue-collar, mechanical energy that counterweights the Irish lyricism. A machine is something you feed. It runs. It doesn't stop. The tension between the two names — one ancient and Celtic, one industrial and Brooklyn — is the same tension that lives inside the collection itself: Homer next to Aesop Rock, Seneca next to Nine Inch Nails.
